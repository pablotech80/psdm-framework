# ADR-2026-07-08-keep-psdm-as-governance-layer-not-ai-observability-platform

Status: `Accepted`
Date: `2026-07-08`
Method: `PTECH SPEC-DRIVEN METHOD`
Artifact Type: `Architecture Decision Record`

## 1. Decision

PSDM will remain a governance framework, not an AI observability platform.

PSDM may define policy, required artifacts, gates, evidence contracts, and lightweight validation, but it will not build native tracing, dashboards, hosted eval pipelines, token telemetry storage, or LangSmith-style runtime observability.

## 2. Context

PSDM is adding AI governance coverage for PII, redaction, cost, latency, tool authorization, evals, and prompt-injection testing. Those concerns naturally touch runtime metrics and model behavior, which creates a scope risk: the framework could drift from governance into full observability, tracing, and eval execution.

That drift would slow the framework, increase dependencies, and weaken the core product direction: risk-scaled governance for AI-assisted software projects.

## 3. Options Considered

1. Keep PSDM as the governance layer and accept evidence from external tools.
2. Build native AI observability, tracing, eval, cost, and latency measurement into PSDM.
3. Only document AI concerns without any structured policy or evidence contract.

## 4. Chosen Option

Keep PSDM as the governance layer and accept evidence from external tools.

## 5. Rationale

This keeps PSDM aligned with its purpose: define what must be governed, when stronger controls are required, and what evidence should exist before risky AI behavior is shipped.

Runtime measurement should remain in application code, CI jobs, logs, APM platforms, provider usage reports, LangSmith, OpenTelemetry, or other specialized systems. PSDM can validate that evidence exists and follows a documented shape, but it should not own the runtime telemetry pipeline.

## 6. Consequences

### Positive

- Keeps the CLI dependency-free and focused.
- Avoids competing with specialized observability and eval platforms.
- Lets teams use their existing telemetry, CI, and AI evaluation tools.
- Preserves PSDM's risk-scaled governance model.

### Negative

- PSDM will not independently prove real p95 latency, token usage, cost, or model quality.
- Teams must provide external evidence when policy requires measured runtime behavior.

### Trade-offs

This optimizes for framework adoption, portability, and architectural clarity. It intentionally does not optimize for end-to-end runtime AI observability inside PSDM itself.

## 7. Validation

- Product direction review with the owner on 2026-07-08.
- Confirmed that adding AI policy fields does not require PSDM to execute LangSmith-style measurement.
- Follow-up work will focus on guardrail templates and optional evidence contracts, not native telemetry collection.
